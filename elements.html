SOME KEY PROJECTS

Detection of C type Solar Flares

I am engaged in a rigorous and ongoing research project focused on the analysis of X-ray light curves derived from cutting-edge solar observation instruments aboard the Chandrayaan 2 orbiter (XSM). My primary research objective is the detection of C type solar flares. To achieve this, I am analysing the lightcurves and parametrising fit-models which can fit the lightcurves and identify C type solar flares. This initiative represents our unwavering commitment to advancing our understanding of the dynamic behavior of our Sun.

Exploring Radiative Processes using Radio Astronomy

My primary objective was to develop a comprehensive understanding of radio astronomy by exploring its foundational concepts and applying them to analyze data collected from radio telescopes using CASA (Common Astronomy Software Applications) software. The project focused on hands-on learning, delving into the principles underlying radio astronomy and utilizing CASA to process and interpret data obtained from radio telescopes. The results were effectively understood through appropriate plots. This report provides a comprehensive learning experience that enabled me to gain proficiency in the fundamental aspects of radio astronomy and its data analysis techniques, with a specific focus on CASA software as a powerful tool for radio astronomical research.

STUDY OF BLACK HOLES IN D-DIMENSIONS

Special Acknowledgments to Prof. Rahul Nigam for guiding me with this project. First, proficient understanding of special relativity, including Lorentz transformations, time dilation, length contraction, and the concept of spacetime was done. Next, I learned how to visualize events using Minkowski and spacetime diagrams, and comprehensive knowledge of the spacetime interval and its invariance. Next, I grasped solid comprehension of gravitation, including the Schwarzschild metric, enabling the analysis of curved spacetime and gravitational effects. This allowed me to understand various important phenomena such as: perturbations in spacetime, Einstein's field equations, etc. You can find an attached report here.
